# PalikaOS User Manual & Workflows

Welcome to PalikaOS! This guide is designed for end-users (Administrators, Ward Officers, Department Heads, and Citizens) to understand how to navigate and use every module within the platform effectively.

---

## 1. Public Citizen Portal

Citizens do not need to log in to access basic public services. They can visit the public-facing homepage of the municipality (e.g., `https://kathmandu.palikaos.com`).

### 1.1 Tracking Services & Complaints (`/citizen/track`)
When a citizen registers a complaint or applies for a service at the ward office, they receive a **Tracking ID**.
*   **Flow:** The citizen enters their Tracking ID on the public portal. The system will display a real-time timeline of the application status (e.g., *Submitted -> Under Review -> Approved*).

### 1.2 Verifying Digital Documents (`/verify/:hash`)
All certificates (Birth, Death, Marriage) generated by PalikaOS include a QR code and a unique cryptographic hash.
*   **Flow:** A third party (like a bank or school) scans the QR code on the certificate. The portal verifies the hash against the blockchain/database to confirm the document is authentic and has not been tampered with.

## 2. Administrator Workflows (Platform & Tenant Admin)

Administrators have full access to configure the municipality's settings, manage staff, and view audit logs.

### 2.1 Customizing Branding (`/settings/branding`)
*   **Flow:** Admins can upload the Municipality's official Logo and select the Primary Brand Color using a color picker. Upon saving, the entire application interface instantly updates to reflect the municipality's branding.

### 2.2 Managing Users & Roles (`/users`, `/roles`)
*   **Flow (Users):** Click "Add User", fill in the staff member's details (Name, Email), and assign them a specific role (e.g., `Ward Officer`, `Health Inspector`).
*   **Flow (Roles):** Admins can create custom roles and specify exact permissions (Read, Write, Delete) for every module in the system to ensure strict access control.

### 2.3 Feature Flags (`/feature-flags`)
*   **Flow:** Administrators can toggle specific modules (like "Tax Engine", "Online Payments", or specific Departments) ON or OFF globally for their municipality instance.

### 2.4 Audit Logs (`/audit-logs`)
*   **Flow:** Provides a read-only, tamper-proof table showing every action taken in the system. Admins can see *who* did *what* and *when* (e.g., "User A deleted Citizen B's record at 10:00 AM").

### 2.5 Billing & Subscriptions (`/billing`)
*   **Flow:** Admins can view their current SaaS subscription plan, check upcoming invoice dates, and pay their municipality's platform fees directly via eSewa.

## 3. Core Operational Modules

These modules form the day-to-day operations for Ward Officers and administrative staff.

### 3.1 Citizen Directory (`/citizens`)
*   **Flow:** Acts as a centralized digital Rolodex for the municipality. Staff can add new citizens, update their contact information, and view their historical interactions with the municipality (taxes paid, complaints filed).

### 3.2 Ghatana Darta / Vital Events (`/ghatana-darta`, `/registrations`)
*   **Flow:** Staff select the event type (Birth, Death, Marriage, Migration), fill out the digitized government form, upload scanned evidence, and submit it. Once approved, the system generates a digitally verifiable certificate with a QR code.

### 3.3 Document Approvals & Sifaris (`/approvals`)
*   **Flow:** This module acts as an inbox for supervisors (like the CAO or Ward Chairman). They view pending documents (like Sifaris or Registrations), review attached evidence, and click **Approve** (which attaches their digital signature) or **Reject** (with a mandatory reason).

### 3.4 Tax Collection & Finance (`/tax-engine`)
*   **Flow:** Staff look up a citizen, select a tax category (Property, Business, Rent), and input the variables. The Tax Engine calculates the exact amount owed based on the municipality's dynamic tax rules. Staff can process the payment (Cash/eSewa) and print a digital receipt.

### 3.5 Service Requests (`/service-requests`)
*   **Flow:** When a citizen requests a service (e.g., "Gravel for local road"), staff log the request here, assign it to a specific department (like Infrastructure), and update the status so the citizen can track it publicly.

### 3.6 Complaints / Hello Mayor (`/complaints`)
*   **Flow:** Similar to Service Requests, but for grievances (e.g., "Broken streetlight"). Complaints can be escalated to higher authorities, and citizens are notified via SMS/Tracking ID when the issue is resolved.

### 3.7 Correspondence / Darta Chalani (`/correspondence`)
*   **Flow:** Digitizes the traditional "Darta Chalani" registry. Incoming official letters (Darta) and outgoing letters (Chalani) are logged here with their dispatch numbers, sender/receiver details, and scanned PDFs for archiving.

## 4. Departmental Modules

PalikaOS includes specialized modules for different municipal departments to track their specific KPIs and records.

### 4.1 Health Department (`/departments/health`)
*   **Flow:** Manage local clinic inventory, track vaccination drives, and record public health statistics.

### 4.2 Education Department (`/departments/education`)
*   **Flow:** Manage public school registries, track scholarship distributions, and monitor student enrollment data across the municipality.

### 4.3 Infrastructure Department (`/departments/infrastructure`)
*   **Flow:** Track the progress of local construction projects (bridges, roads, ward offices), manage contractor details, and monitor budget allocation vs. expenditure for projects.

### 4.4 Agriculture Department (`/departments/agriculture`)
*   **Flow:** Register local farmers, track the distribution of subsidized seeds/fertilizers, and monitor local agricultural output.

### 4.5 Disaster Management (`/departments/disaster-management`)
*   **Flow:** A critical dashboard for logging natural disasters (floods, landslides), managing emergency relief funds, and tracking the distribution of relief materials to affected citizens.

## 5. Keyboard Shortcuts & Accessibility

PalikaOS is built for speed and efficiency, allowing data entry clerks to work without taking their hands off the keyboard.

### 5.1 The Command Palette
At any time, from any page, press `Cmd + K` (Mac) or `Ctrl + K` (Windows) to open the **Command Palette**.
*   **Flow:** Type what you want to do (e.g., "Add Citizen", "Settings", "Health Department"). Use the **Up/Down Arrow Keys** to navigate the list, and press **Enter** to instantly jump to that page.

### 5.2 Keyboard Navigation
All complex components (Dropdowns, Multi-Selects, Modals) can be fully navigated using the keyboard:
*   Press **Tab** to move between input fields.
*   Press **Arrow Keys** to navigate dropdown menus or tables.
*   Press **ESC** to close any open modal, menu, or command palette.
